Freya's Math Revision Notes 2025


Factors

  • Factor: A divisor of a number.
  • Factors of 8 are:
    • 1, 2, 4, and 8
  • Factors of 12 are:
    • 1, 2, 3, 4, 6 and 12
  • Common factors of 8 and 12 are:
    • 1, 2 and 4
  • The highest common factor 8 and 12 is:
    • $$ hcf(8,12)=\color{lime}{4} $$

Multiples

  • Multiples of 8 are:
    • 8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, 56, 64, 72, ...
  • Multiples of 12 are:
    • 12, 24, 36, 48, 60, 72, ...
  • The common multiples of 8 and 12 are:
    • 24, 48, 72, ...
  • The least common multiple 8 and 12 is:
    • $$ lcm(8,12)=\color{red}{24} $$

Link between the LCM and HCF

  • The lcm and hcf are linked, eg:
    • $$ lcm(8,12)=\frac{8 \times 12}{hcf(8,12)} = \frac{96}{4} = 24 $$
  • So, the lcm(11, 17) is
    • $$ lcm(11,17)=\frac{11 \times 17}{hcf(11,17)} = \frac{187}{1} = 187 $$
  • and the lcm(8, 300) is
    • $$ lcm(8,30)=\frac{8 \times 30}{hcf(8,30)} = \frac{240}{2} = 120 $$

Prime Numbers

  • A prime number is:
    • a number with exactly two factors (1 and itself)
  • First five primes are
    • 2, 3, 5, 7, 11,...
  • Any whole number (integer) may be written
    • as a unique product of primes
  • 12 may be written as
    • $$ 12 = 2^2 \times 3^1 = 2^2 \times 3 $$
  • 8 may be written as
    • $$ 8 = 2^3 $$
  • 100 may be written as
    • $$ 100 = 2^2 \times 5^2 $$

Prime Numbers - hcf and lcm

  • Prime numbers can be used to calculate the lcm and hcf of two numbers.
  • $$ 12 = 2^2 \times 3 = 2^\color{red}{2} \times 3^\color{lime}{1} \\ 8 = 2^3 = 2^\color{cyan}{3} \times 3^\color{yellow}{0} $$
  • The highest common factor can then be calculated as
    • $$ \begin{aligned} hcf(8,12) =& 2^{min(\color{red}{2}, \color{cyan}{3})} \times 3^{min(\color{lime}{1}, \color{yellow}{0})} \\ =& 2^{\color{red}{2}} \times 3^{\color{yellow}{0}} \\ =& 4 \end{aligned} $$
  • The lowest common multiple can then be calculated as
    • $$ \begin{aligned} lcm(8,12) =& 2^{max(\color{red}{2}, \color{cyan}{3})} \times 3^{max(\color{lime}{1}, \color{yellow}{0})} \\ =& 2^{\color{cyan}{3}} \times 3^{\color{lime}{1}} \\ =& 24 \end{aligned} $$

Prime Numbers - hcf and lcm

  • What is lcm(30, 100)?
    • $$ \begin{aligned} &30 = 2^1 \times 3^\color{lime}{1} \times 5^1 \text{ and } 100= 2^\color{red}{2} \times 3^0 \times 5^\color{cyan}{2} \\ \implies& lcm(30, 100) = 2^\color{red}{2} \times 3^\color{lime}{1} \times 5^\color{cyan}{2} = 300 \end{aligned} $$
  • What is lcm(16, 20)?
    • $$ \begin{aligned} &16 = 2^\color{lime}{4} \times 5^0 \text{ and } 20= 2^2 \times 5^\color{cyan}{1} \\ \implies& lcm(16, 20) = 2^\color{lime}{4} \times 5^\color{cyan}{1} = 80 \end{aligned} $$
  • What is hcf(16, 28)?
    • $$ \begin{aligned} &16 = 2^4 \times 7^\color{red}{0} \text{ and } 28= 2^\color{lime}{2} \times 7^1 \\ \implies& hcf(16, 28) = 2^\color{lime}{2} \times 7^\color{red}{0} = 4 \end{aligned} $$
